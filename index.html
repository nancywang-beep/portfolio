About Me

I am a Master's student focused on the intersection of semiconductor fabrication and electrical validation. With hands-on experience in cleanroom microfabrication and power electronics testing, I bridge the gap between physical device design and real-world parametric performance. My background includes a one-year internship at HPE developing next-gen power solutions and research-driven microfabrication at Purdue’s Birck Nanotechnology Center.

Key Projects

Test Pattern Design & Fabrication

Birck Nanotechnology Center | Purdue University

Problem: Need for robust test structures to monitor semiconductor process uniformity and extract sheet/contact resistance.

  • Designed full-wafer layouts in KLayout featuring serpentine resistors, interdigitated comb, CBKRs, and MOSCAPs.
  • Executed end-to-end fabrication flows: spin coating, mask alignment, photolithography, and etching.
  • Conducted device-level characterization using probe stations or microscopy to extract variability metrics.

Outcome: Characterized process sensitivity and repeatability to support future 8-inch wafer integration.

Next-Gen Power MOSFET Evaluation

Hewlett Packard Enterprise | AC Power Engineering Intern

Problem: Assessing the feasibility of 3rd-generation semiconductor materials for HPE Gen12 server power supplies.

  • Supported PCB design and bring-up through schematic/layout review.
  • Conducted bench-level electrical validation and troubleshooting of power electronics using oscilloscopes, current probes, differential probes, and power analyzers.
  • Performed PCB rework and assembly, including fine-pitch and small-package SMD soldering (e.g., 0201–0805) for debugging and design iteration.

Outcome: Provided data-driven insights that guided future power device selection and platform optimization.
